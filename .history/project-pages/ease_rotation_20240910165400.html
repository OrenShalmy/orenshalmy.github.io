Animated Rotation with easing
Rotate a video from an angle to another angle, over time with easing in/out
Code:
                startAngle=10 # Angle to start the rotation from
                  input='/path/to/file.png'
                  output='/path/to/output.mp4'
                  start_angle=0
                  end_angle=-90
                  anim_duration=2
                  easing_factor=-5
                  
                  ffmpeg -loop 1 -i $input -t $anim_duration -filter_complex \
                  "[0:v]rotate=angle=(${start_angle} * PI/180) - (((1 - exp(${easing_factor} * (t / ${anim_duration}))) / (1 - exp(${easing_factor}))) * (${start_angle} - ${end_angle}) * PI/180)[rotated]" \
                  -map "[rotated]" -y $output
Get your console ready...
This creates rotation animation with ease in/out.
Negative values in easing_factor will ease the animation out.
Positive values in easing_factor will ease the animation in.

On line 8 | Call ffmpeg binary
On line 9 | Call the input video
On line 10 | Starting the filter complex chain
On line 11 | Call the first input video - [0]. It starts the rotation from the starting angle and ends the rotation at the desired angle, over the specified time. Name the link [out].
On line 12 | Call the filter chain output called [out]. Overwrite output file.
